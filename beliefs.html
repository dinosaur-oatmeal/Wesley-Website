What We Believe

At the UTA Wesley, we affirm that all people are created in God’s own image and deserving of respect, dignity, and justice. We embrace and celebrate diversity in all its forms.

God

We affirm God revealed to us in three persons—Creator, Christ, and Holy Spirit. God is all good, all powerful, and all knowing, seeking relationship with all of creation.

Jesus Christ

We affirm the incarnation of Christ—fully human and fully divine. Through his life, death, resurrection, and ascension, we are graciously afforded life eternal and the opportunity to live into God’s plan for forgiveness.

The Holy Spirit

We affirm the presence of the Holy Spirit in our lives, working in ways seen and unseen to lead us toward God, to know God’s will, and to sense God’s presence and love.

The Bible

We believe in the centrality of Holy Scriptures. God speaks to us through scripture, enabling us to better understand Jesus Christ and feel the Holy Spirit move within our being.

The Reign of God

We understand God’s reign as both now and present, and that which is to come. It is God’s work in our lives today that draws us toward the culmination of God’s plan in the future.

The Church

We as followers of Christ are called be active in the ministry of Good News. When we come together as one body, collaborating with the Creator, the fullness of the Reign of God draws nearer.

Our Wesleyan Heritage

As a United Methodist Collegiate Ministry, we are shaped by the teachings of John Wesley. We understand the centrality of scripture as supported by tradition, our God-given reason, and the experience of God transforming us all.

Radically Inclusive

The UTA Wesley is a space where people of all nations, races, genders, orientations, citizenship statuses, and theological understandings are invited together. Each person, as a beloved creation of God, will be treated with dignity, respect, and radical grace.
